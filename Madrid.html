My friend from Madrid

I visited Madrid for the simple pleasure of following the footsteps of my good friend Santi. Who on that trip I remember referred to himself and his friends as "deadbeats". Something that surprised me because for me they were freer than any people who follow all the social norms. They were not perfect; as no one is, but they were content and at times even happier than anyone I had ever met. I met Santi in college. He gave me the impression of being a person who enjoy life in a way that I had never allowed myself: without guilt and with complete freedom. The first year in college we had the same itinerary, we became inseparable at that time and I liked spending time with him because, unlike me, he didn't know any shyness. He had charisma and somehow knew where the parties were at all times. I considered my friendship with him part of my college education. Several years later I decided to visit him at his house in Spain. That's where I took these photos. On that visit I discovered why I enjoy spending time with this friend: It was because I identified with him, for me he is the most genuine person —of my age— that I know. I don't know many people who live their lives as they please or who have the ability to always do what they want without remorse. I admired that freedom because it is something I loose all the time and when I see him–he reminds me why is healthy to give yourself the freedom to do what you want: because it puts you at peace with yourself.

Madrid, 2019
